Meetup Sponsorship

Enjoy the great food and resources we provide at the JavaScript meetup? As a sponsor of the Edmonton JavaScript meetup there are lots of ways you can help, and benefit. So do the right thing and sponsor us today!

tl;dr: Supplying food and drink is the main way most groups sponsor our meetup, and we need at least one sponsor a month to make this happen. We book sponsorship up to six months in advance, and take payment in goods and services. No cash, please. Sponsor us now!

Need Help?

Contact us on Twitter at @edmontonjs, or any of the meetup organizers listed on our homepage.

Why Sponsor Us

Every month The Edmonton JavaScipt Meetup provides a venue, speakers, food and other resources for the local JavaScript community. Beyond this the meetup connects you, the people and organizations that make up the local JavaScript scene, with the successes, stories, and opportunities you care about. Doing all this takes the support of a team of volunteers and a dedicated community of sponsors. We wouldn't exist without you.

There are many benefits to sponsoring the meetup.

First, your given the opportunity to get a special mention at each meetup you sponsor. We do our best to make sure our members know who they have to thank for the meetup each month.

Second, you get a chance to place your companies logo on the homepage of our meetup for the duration of the year you sponsored us. We people don't always make it to every meetup, and we want to show our gratitude for your support all year long.

How To Sponsor

You first need to decide how you'd like to sponsor us. There are many ways to do this such as:

  • Supplying food and drink
  • Speaking at the meetup
  • Supporting a professional speaker
  • Picking up our hosting fees
  • Providing a venue for a special Exchange.js event
  • Donating books, licenses or other prizes

Supplying food and drink is the main way most groups sponsor our meetup, and we need at least one sponsor a month to make this happen. It's a great way to get a special mention at a meetup, as we make sure members know who's supplying the refreshments. Jump ahead to the Food and Drink section for details.

We're also interested in creative suggestions, so get in touch if you'd like to share your ideas.

Once you've decided how you'd like to sponsor us fill out our sponsorship form and we'll get in touch with more details.

Food and Drink

Sponsoring food and drink typically costs between $100-150 per meetup, depending on the number of attendees and the kind of food you choose to provide. Popular food choices are catered sandwiches, wraps, and pizza.

When you decided to sponsor, please fill out our sponsorship form and let us know your preferences. To make sure that all interested sponsors get a chance to participate twice a year, in February and June, we collect the list of sponsors and divide them out between the interested parties. We then clear the list of waiting sponsors and start again.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to sponsor the meetup to share a job opportunity?

No. Though we encourage you to consider sponsoring the meetup, we welcome all job posting regardless of whether you sponsor or not. That said, if you're finding good people from the meetup, consider sharing the good fortune and giving sponsorship a go.

Why don't you take cash donations?

We've considered taking cash donations, but doing so would require registering as a not-for-profit and paying for a bank account. We'd rather spend that time and money making the meetup awesome, so ask that all donations are provided in goods and services instead.

Why do you book sponsorships in advance?

Booking sponsorship in advance gives us the structure we need to plan out food and other logistics in advance, knowing that we'll be able to cover the costs. By only doing this a few times a year, instead of every month, we can focus on the important tasks of arranging meetups and events.

Why don't you book sponsorship more than six months in advance?

Arranging the sponsorship in advance reduces the amount of time we spend chasing down sponsors, but we recognize that planning too far in advance is impractical for some organizations. Six months is a good balance between planning ahead, and not waiting to long for a sponsorship opportunity.

What kind of options are available for sponsoring the meetup?

There are many options available from food and refreshments, to sponsoring speakers and talks.

What if I can't afford to sponsor with goods or services? Are there other ways I can help?

Yes! If you'd like to help the meetup but don't have the funds to do so, consider doing a talk or contacting a member of the exec to let them know you're interested.
